The dumbbell benchpress can be used as a variation to the barbell benchpress, and the same aspects for form can generally be applied:

  • Feet positioned under the bench
  • Shoulders and head drawn back into the bench
  • Avoid explosive movements for hypertrophy and muscle stimulation in particular

However, beyond exercise variation for the sake of it, an advantage to the dumbbell benchpress is that it allows for a greater range of motion. With that in mind:

  • Expect to use far lighter weight than you're capable of pressing
  • Lower the dumbbells as far as you can (lower than you would for a barbell)
  • During the lock out of the movement, you can press outward (as opposed to touching the dumbbells together at the top) in order to maintain tension on your chest.
